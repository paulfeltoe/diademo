Sinusitis

Overview Care Plan Chat Team Links

Condition Summary

Initial Complaint: Severe nasal congestion, facial pressure, and headache for past week.

Key Findings: Acute bacterial sinusitis with moderate inflammation of maxillary and frontal sinuses.

Current Status: Responding to antibiotics, symptoms improving gradually.

Treatment Plan: 10-day course of antibiotics, nasal steroids, and supportive care.

Quick Actions

Read About Sinusitis Book Follow-up Nasal Spray Guide Track Symptoms

Your Treatment Plan

Prescribed Medications

  • Amoxicillin-Clavulanate: 875/125mg tablet twice daily for 10 days
  • Fluticasone Nasal Spray: 2 sprays in each nostril once daily
  • Saline Nasal Spray: As needed for congestion
  • Important: Complete entire course of antibiotics even if symptoms improve

Symptom Management

  • Pain Relief: Acetaminophen or ibuprofen as needed for pain/fever
  • Nasal Irrigation: Use saline rinse 2-3 times daily
  • Steam Inhalation: 10-15 minutes, 3-4 times daily
  • Hydration: Drink 8-10 glasses of water daily

Home Care Instructions

  • Sleep Position: Elevate head with extra pillows
  • Air Quality: Use a humidifier at night, clean regularly
  • Environment: Avoid smoke, strong fragrances, and known allergens
  • Rest: Get adequate sleep and avoid strenuous activities
  • Temperature: Apply warm compress to face for 5-10 minutes, 3-4 times daily

Follow-up Plan

  • Virtual Check-in: Scheduled for 5 days from initial visit
  • Monitoring: Track temperature and symptoms daily
  • Duration: Expect improvement within 3-5 days of treatment
  • Recovery: Full resolution typically within 10-14 days

When to Seek Immediate Care

  • Severe headache or facial pain that worsens
  • Fever above 102°F (39°C) that persists
  • Vision changes, eye swelling, or severe redness
  • Symptoms worsen after initial improvement
  • Neck stiffness or severe confusion
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath

Your Migraine Management Plan

Prescribed Medications

  • Preventive Treatment:
    • Propranolol 40mg twice daily
    • Magnesium supplement 400mg daily
    • Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin) 400mg daily
  • Acute Treatment:
    • Sumatriptan 50mg at onset of migraine (max 2 doses/24hrs)
    • Ondansetron 4mg as needed for nausea
  • Important: Do not use acute medication more than 2-3 times per week to prevent rebound headaches

Trigger Management

  • Sleep: Maintain consistent sleep schedule (7-9 hours)
  • Diet: Avoid identified triggers:
    • Aged cheeses and processed meats
    • Caffeine (limit to 200mg daily)
    • Artificial sweeteners
    • Red wine and other alcoholic beverages
  • Environment:
    • Reduce bright/flickering lights exposure
    • Minimize screen time with blue light filter
    • Avoid strong perfumes and chemical odors
  • Stress: Practice stress reduction techniques daily

Lifestyle Modifications

  • Exercise:
    • 30 minutes of low-impact exercise 3-4 times weekly
    • Regular neck and shoulder stretches
    • Gentle yoga or tai chi recommended
  • Hydration: Minimum 2L water daily
  • Meals: Regular meal times, avoid skipping meals
  • Relaxation:
    • Daily meditation or deep breathing exercises
    • Progressive muscle relaxation before bed
    • Regular massage therapy sessions

Monitoring & Follow-up

  • Daily Tracking:
    • Log migraine frequency, intensity, and duration
    • Record potential triggers and medication use
    • Track sleep patterns and stress levels
  • Follow-up Schedule:
    • Virtual check-in every 2 weeks
    • Full evaluation every 3 months
    • Medication review every 6 months

When to Seek Immediate Care

  • Sudden, severe headache ("thunderclap" headache)
  • Migraine with confusion or difficulty speaking
  • New neurological symptoms (weakness, vision changes)
  • Fever with severe headache and neck stiffness
  • Headache following head injury
  • Migraine lasting more than 72 hours despite treatment
